1 Hour of Smoke Is Enough for Real Barbecue

The Ballad of a Barbecue Rustler
Working Draft
1

LOW ANGLE — CHRIS (50mm anamorphic, 1.33x)

Chris fills the frame, looking straight down the lens. On his last word, his hand finds the smoker lid at the bottom of frame.

CHRIS

Great barbecue is supposed to take all day.

MATCH CUT TO:

2

INSIDE THE SMOKER

From black, the lid lifts and bright sunlight cracks through billowing smoke. As it clears, an almost-black pork shoulder snaps into focus. Three-quarter top-down, a gentle push in.

CHRIS (V.O.)

But I finished this shoulder in one hour — by reverse smoking.

3

WIDE — CHRIS, BACKLIT

Hard sun behind him, smoke hanging in the air. Chris pats the smoke and dust off himself, then strides out of frame — leaving a ghost of smoke where he stood. No dialogue; the gag lands on its own.

4

CLOSE — CHRIS TO CAMERA

Tight and intimate now. Chris levels with us.

CHRIS

It lets you leave the hours of smoke and sweat behind—

5

INSERT — THE BARK

Close on the crust: deep mahogany, glistening, a faint smoke ring at the cut edge.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—and make barbecue that's the best in your neighborhood—

6

EXTERIOR — DUSK TO NIGHT

Smoke trails up off the smoker into a darkening sky; the light falls to night.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—by beating the stall while you sleep.

7

MEDIUM — CHRIS TO CAMERA

Chris faces the lens. A graph animates in the air beside him — a smoke-development curve climbing toward an optimal window.

CHRIS

And I'll bring new data—

8

MODERNIST CUISINE — VOLUME 2

Chris's hand slides Volume 2 off the shelf.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—what even we missed back in 2008, writing Modernist Cuisine—

9

INSERT — PAGE FLIP (low, raking angle)

A dramatic raking angle as the pages riffle past, fast — until they settle on a stark black divider page, white type: OPTIMAL TEMPERATURE FOR SMOKING.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—on what really builds deep, pit-smoked flavor and dark, mahogany bark.

DISSOLVE THROUGH BLACK TO:

10

INSIDE THE EGG — TIMELAPSE

The black page resolves into meat transforming on the grate — surface darkening, smoke streaming. The camera dollies back to reveal the whole scene is a cutaway cross-section of a Big Green Egg, which then animates shut.

11

BACK TO CHRIS

Chris, back to the lens.

CHRIS

And as a bonus, I'll share a technique that lets you do all of this without any special equipment.

12

KITCHEN — NIGHT

Hard cut to a bare, raw shoulder under kitchen light. Chris's hands set it down.

CHRIS (V.O.)

It starts the night before.
