1 Hour of Smoke Is Enough for Real Barbecue

The Ballad of a Barbecue Rustler
Working Draft
1

INSIDE THE SMOKER — THE LID OPENS

FADE IN: Solid black — inside the smoker. The lid opens, bright sunlight cracking in through thick smoke; as it clears, an amazing-looking, almost-black pork shoulder comes into focus. High three-quarter angle, looking down on it.

CHRIS (V.O.)

This pork shoulder has only smoked for one hour—

2

LOW ANGLE — ON CHRIS

The camera cranes up off the pork to find Chris over the smoker, filling the frame. He looks straight down the lens, sly.

CHRIS

—and it should have no business tasting like authentic low-and-slow barbecue.

3

INT. SALOON — CHRIS ENTERS

Chris lifts the shoulder off the smoker and shoulders in through the batwing doors. A low tracking shot leads him across the room; the murmur drops and weathered heads turn.

CHRIS (V.O.)

So I'm serving it to some of the best pitmasters in the West—

4

AT THE PITMASTERS' TABLE

He sets the shoulder down before them and steps back, hands open. A held beat.

5

CLOSE — THE PITMASTERS

Push in on their faces — arms crossed, a squint, a slow shake of the head.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—to see if they can catch me cheating.

6

REVERSE — ON CHRIS

He meets their doubt with a small, knowing look.

CHRIS (V.O.)

Because if they can't tell—

7

TRACKING — CHRIS TO THE DOORS

He slaps the smoke off his chest — two quick pats, a puff of woodsmoke — turns on his heel and struts for the batwing doors. The pitmasters stare after him.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—you can leave all the sweat and smoke behind—

8

EXT. SALOON — THE STREET

He pushes out into the daylight, the doors swinging behind him, and crosses toward the smoker.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—and make the best barbecue in your town—

9

ON THE SMOKER

He saunters past; the camera lets him leave frame and pushes into the smoke until it fills the screen.

CHRIS (V.O.)

—with only an hour of real effort.
